there are two types of hacking:-

1.Ethical Hacking:

Ethical hacking, also known as "white hat hacking," involvescybersecurity professionals or individuals with permission
testing and assessing the security of websites, networks, and systems. Their goal is to identify vulnerabilities and
weaknesses in a system before malicious hackers can exploit them.Ethical hackers typically have explicit permission
to conduct security assessments and are bound by strict ethical guidelines and legal frameworks.Common techniques
and tools used by ethical hackers include penetration testing,vulnerability scanning, and security audits. The objective
is to help organizationsimprove their security posture by addressing vulnerabilities and enhancing their defenses.

2.Illegal Hacking:

Illegal hacking, often referred to as "black hat hacking," involves unauthorized access to computer systems, websites, and
networks with malicious intent. This can include stealing sensitive data, defacing websites, distributing malware, or in other
cybercriminal activities. Black hat hackers operate outside the boundaries of the law, and their actions are considered illegal
and unethical.Engaging in illegal hacking activities can lead to criminal charges, including fines and imprisonment. It also
causes harm to individuals and organizations, often resulting in financial losses and reputational damage.
